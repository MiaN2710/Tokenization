## Motion Research
Kinethic investigates motion as data, authorship, and embodied knowledge. Our research explores how human movement, captured through dance, gesture, sport, or daily activity, can be encoded without losing its origin or context. We work with choreographers, performers, and researchers to understand how bodies generate meaning rather than mere metrics. Motion data is treated as a living record that is expressive, ethical, and relational. By analyzing movement across artistic, technological, and social domains, we aim to create frameworks where every recorded motion carries provenance, consent, and cultural specificity. In this way, motion capture becomes a regenerative form of knowledge rather than an extractive process.

## Blockchain
Our blockchain research focuses on designing performer-first infrastructures for motion data. We develop tokenization protocols that embed provenance, royalties, and licensing terms directly into motion files. Smart contracts automate fair compensation and ensure compliance. Each motion dataset becomes a traceable digital asset that guarantees ownership, consent, and attribution throughout its entire lifecycle. This approach responds to the opacity of current data economies by creating a clear chain of accountability from performer to application. Beyond security and payment automation, our blockchain framework establishes a new ethical standard for creative data and transforms motion from raw input into a governed and equitable resource.

## AI Model
We are developing methods for ethically training machine learning and artificial intelligence models on consented, provenance-secured motion datasets. Our goal is to build generative systems capable of classifying, predicting, and producing movement without detaching from the embodied labor that created them. This involves defining transparent data pipelines, bias-aware learning protocols, and model architectures that register authorship within the training process itself. By embedding ethical metadata into training datasets, we aim to prevent the erasure of human contribution and to foster accountability in embodied AI. Our research envisions AI as a collaborator that learns responsibly from the body’s intelligence.

## Archive
The Ethical Motion Archive is conceived as a living repository for performer-owned motion data. It preserves gesture, choreography, and embodied movement as both cultural and economic knowledge. Each archived dataset carries metadata linking it to its origin, license, and consent conditions, ensuring transparency and accountability. The archive functions as a cultural memory infrastructure and as a technical platform where artists, researchers, and developers can access ethically sourced movement data for study or creative use. In contrast to extractive data banks, the Ethical Motion Archive supports equitable circulation in which performers retain agency, visibility, and revenue over the afterlife of their captured motion.

## Publications
Kinethic’s publications connect research, policy, and artistic practice around ethics, movement, and intelligent systems. Our writings appear in academic journals, cultural platforms, and white papers that address AI ethics, choreography, and data provenance. Key works include Kinethics: Authorship, Ownership, and Transparency in AI Choreography and the forthcoming article on Motion Ethics. Through these outputs, we share theoretical and technical insights, contribute to European ethical frameworks, and shape public discussion on embodied intelligence. Each publication reflects our commitment to transparency and turns complex interdisciplinary research into accessible knowledge for artists, policymakers, and developers.
